Theory of action

Giving families public funds for private schools increases match quality and competitive pressure.

Years / scale

2000s onward; federal/state/local

Category

Choice

Target population

Students eligible for scholarships

Inputs changed

Scholarship amount, eligibility, private school participation, admissions lotteries, accountability rules

Outputs measured

Test scores, parent satisfaction, safety, graduation, college enrollment

Evaluation designs

Lottery/RCT designs; administrative follow-up

Normalization problems

Voucher effects depend on participating schools and accountability rules; compare offers (ITT) and actual attendance (TOT).
