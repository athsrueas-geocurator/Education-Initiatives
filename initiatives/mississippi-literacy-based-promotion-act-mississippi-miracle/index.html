Literacy

Mississippi Literacy-Based Promotion Act / 'Mississippi miracle'

Mississippi is one of the most cited recent literacy success cases, but the causal mechanism is a policy bundle rather than one isolated program.

Promising causal/quasiDescriptive

Theory of action

Pair early screening, teacher training, coaching, interventions, and third-grade promotion rules to improve early reading.

Years / scale

2013 onward; state

Category

Literacy

Target population

Mississippi K-3 students and educators

Inputs changed

K-3 literacy instruction, phonics emphasis, reading coaches, intervention plans, retention/promotion threshold

Outputs measured

Third-grade reading pass rates, NAEP grade 4 reading, state ELA, retention and exemptions

Evaluation designs

State trends; NAEP comparison; RD/quasi evidence on retention

Normalization problems

NAEP gains are state-level and bundled; retention changes who appears in grade 4, so researchers analyze both policy components and cohort composition.

Related dichotomies

Whole language / balanced literacy vs phonics / Science of Reading
