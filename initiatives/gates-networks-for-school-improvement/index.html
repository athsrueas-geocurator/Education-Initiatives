Gates Networks for School Improvement

The final evaluation found promising effects on some targeted outcomes, with engagement and CI fidelity tied to stronger results.

Promising causal/quasiRCT

Theory of action

Networked continuous improvement helps schools identify problems, test changes, and spread effective practices.

Years / scale

2018-2026; nearly 800 schools across networks

Category

School organization/governance

Target population

Middle/high school transitions and postsecondary readiness

Inputs changed

Intermediary support, CI cycles, shared aims, data use, student voice, cross-school learning

Outputs measured

9th-grade on-track, course failures, attendance, FAFSA, postsecondary enrollment

Evaluation designs

RCT and quasi evaluation; implementation study

Normalization problems

Impacts vary by network; researchers compare schools with stronger engagement and use targeted outcomes rather than broad test scores only.

Related dichotomies

Choice/markets vs common public system
